  7: Pret a Manger
  8: Overview
  9: Pret A Manger, a prominent coffee chain operating in the UK, France, and the US, offered users the ability to view the menu and make payments through their app, with transactions converting into loyalty points. At Jio, we engaged in discussions with Pret to extend and enhance their loyalty program as part of their expansion into the Indian market, where they partnered with us to grow their presence.
  10: Approach
  11: Initially, during our interactions, we were introduced to the culture and methodology employed in the Pret loyalty app. Our design team (comprising of myself, Anjan, and Sanskruti) carried out preliminary research and set benchmarks to evaluate how we could roll out the Pret Loyalty program in India.
  12: Pret a Manger UK
  13: Here, I'm showcasing app snippets from Pret UK. The current functionality is limited to viewing the menu, without an option for ordering through the app. Additionally, the menu design restricts users to specific categories rather than providing a universal discovery experience.
  14: Initial Research and Benchmarking
  15: We held the belief that a basic menu and payment system won't suffice in this scenario. Given that we already boast a highly efficient and resilient UPI system in India, shifting to app-based payment could be a challenge. Therefore, we felt obligated to offer something extra. This led us to direct our research towards Indian applications that incorporate loyalty programs, such as Dominos, McD, Starbucks, and Third Wave Coffee.
  16: Initial Ideations and Proposals
  17: We began with the initial ideation phase, focusing on outlining the project approach, defining potential deliverables, and identifying supporting evidence and theoretical frameworks. This stage involved collaborative brainstorming to establish a strategic direction, aligning our goals with Pret's business objectives while ensuring a thorough understanding of user needs and market opportunities.
  18: Initial Findings
  19: Competitors like Domino's, Starbucks, and Third Wave Coffee, along with indirect players such as Zomato and Swiggy, all offered robust order placement experiences, complete with seamless customization options and well-established delivery infrastructures. It was clear that Pret needed to align with these industry standards to remain competitive and meet evolving customer expectations. Recognizing the gap, we initiated strategic ideation sessions aimed at enhancing Pret's capabilities beyond mere menu viewing. Our objective was to incorporate a comprehensive order placement feature. Through well-structured proposals and demonstrating market potential, we successfully convinced the stakeholders to embrace our vision and move forward with expanding the app's functionalities to meet customer demands and surpass the competition.
  20: This project is currently a work in progress. While the detailed screens for the loyalty program are yet to be developed, we have already mapped out the overall user flow. The business team is actively working on defining deliverables and crafting the user stories, keeping us closely aligned throughout the process. Once the deliverables are finalized and we have a clear direction on how the loyalty program will be represented, we will move forward with designing the specific interfaces, ensuring that the visual experience aligns seamlessly with the business strategy and user expectations.
  21: Finalised Screens
  22: Design Challenges Faced
  23: Customization Handling
  24: Benchmarking Data
  25: : Conducted benchmarking for Domino's, Starbucks, Third Wave Coffee, Zomato, and Swiggy to understand customization handling.
  26: Challenges
  27: : Observed that parts of the UX were compromised, especially with repetitive customizations. Issues included key elements being hidden inside CTAs or displayed in fonts and layouts that were not user-friendly, affecting usability.
  28: Solutions
  29: Maintained consistent layout for customization options, providing sufficient space and legible font sizes to facilitate quicker actions.
  30: Implemented a prominent CTA (confirm button) immediately upon any changes being made.
