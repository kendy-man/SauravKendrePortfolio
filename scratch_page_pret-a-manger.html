Overview

Overview

Overview

Pret A Manger, a prominent coffee chain operating in the UK, France, and the US, offered users the ability to view the menu and make payments through their app, with transactions converting into loyalty points. At Jio, we engaged in discussions with Pret to extend and enhance their loyalty program as part of their expansion into the Indian market, where they partnered with us to grow their presence.

Approach

Approach

Approach

Initially, during our interactions, we were introduced to the culture and methodology employed in the Pret loyalty app. Our design team (comprising of myself, Anjan, and Sanskruti) carried out preliminary research and set benchmarks to evaluate how we could roll out the Pret Loyalty program in India.

Pret a Manger UK

Here, I'm showcasing app snippets from Pret UK. The current functionality is limited to viewing the menu, without an option for ordering through the app. Additionally, the menu design restricts users to specific categories rather than providing a universal discovery experience.

Initial Research and Benchmarking

We held the belief that a basic menu and payment system won't suffice in this scenario. Given that we already boast a highly efficient and resilient UPI system in India, shifting to app-based payment could be a challenge. Therefore, we felt obligated to offer something extra. This led us to direct our research towards Indian applications that incorporate loyalty programs, such as Dominos, McD, Starbucks, and Third Wave Coffee.

Initial Ideations and Proposals

We began with the initial ideation phase, focusing on outlining the project approach, defining potential deliverables, and identifying supporting evidence and theoretical frameworks. This stage involved collaborative brainstorming to establish a strategic direction, aligning our goals with Pret's business objectives while ensuring a thorough understanding of user needs and market opportunities.

Initial Findings

Initial Findings

Initial Findings

Competitors like Domino's, Starbucks, and Third Wave Coffee, along with indirect players such as Zomato and Swiggy, all offered robust order placement experiences, complete with seamless customization options and well-established delivery infrastructures. It was clear that Pret needed to align with these industry standards to remain competitive and meet evolving customer expectations. Recognizing the gap, we initiated strategic ideation sessions aimed at enhancing Pret's capabilities beyond mere menu viewing. Our objective was to incorporate a comprehensive order placement feature. Through well-structured proposals and demonstrating market potential, we successfully convinced the stakeholders to embrace our vision and move forward with expanding the app's functionalities to meet customer demands and surpass the competition.

This project is currently a work in progress. While the detailed screens for the loyalty program are yet to be developed, we have already mapped out the overall user flow. The business team is actively working on defining deliverables and crafting the user stories, keeping us closely aligned throughout the process. Once the deliverables are finalized and we have a clear direction on how the loyalty program will be represented, we will move forward with designing the specific interfaces, ensuring that the visual experience aligns seamlessly with the business strategy and user expectations.

Design Challenges Faced

Design Challenges Faced

Design Challenges Faced

  1. Customization Handling

Benchmarking Data: Conducted benchmarking for Domino's, Starbucks, Third Wave Coffee, Zomato, and Swiggy to understand customization handling.

Challenges: Observed that parts of the UX were compromised, especially with repetitive customizations. Issues included key elements being hidden inside CTAs or displayed in fonts and layouts that were not user-friendly, affecting usability.

Solutions: a). Maintained consistent layout for customization options, providing sufficient space and legible font sizes to facilitate quicker actions. b). Implemented a prominent CTA (confirm button) immediately upon any changes being made. c). Incorporated a "cancel" action via a cross mark or by clicking outside the customization zone, accompanied by a toast message for feedback.

  1. Store Locator

Benchmarking Data: Referenced store locator features in Domino's, Starbucks, Third Wave Coffee, Zomato, and Swiggy.

Challenges: As only a store pickup option was available, functionalities such as saved addresses, user location detection, and detailed location inputs had to be restricted. Displaying store information became challenging as we did not mandate location access. Additionally, managing store opening and closing times for order placement, with the requirement that users be physically present at the store, added complexity.

  1. Integration of Pret UK's Design System

Challenges: Two months into the project, we had to incorporate Pret UK's design system, including changes to colors, fonts, and atomic components.

Solutions: To ensure consistency in design architecture while minimizing confusion and managing timelines effectively, a short presentation was prepared to align stakeholders with the process, detailing the scope and time required for this design system transition.

In conclusion, the journey of enhancing Pret A Manger's app experience has been both challenging and rewarding. By addressing customization complexities, simplifying the store locator functionality, and incorporating Pret UK's design system, we have navigated significant design hurdles to create a user-friendly and cohesive solution. These efforts aim to elevate Pret's digital presence, keeping it competitive and aligned with modern customer expectations. Moving forward, our focus remains on refining these solutions, incorporating stakeholder feedback, and continuously improving the user experience to ensure a seamless, intuitive journey for Pret’s expanding customer base in India.
