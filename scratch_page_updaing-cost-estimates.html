Intuitive Cost Updating

Intuitive Cost Updating

Overview

Overview

Overview

Shippers on our platform request shipment quotes. These estimates are generated automatically based on rate charts configured by Dispatchers during onboarding or when launching new services. Originally, estimates were read-only. Any correction or adjustment triggered manual back-and-forth between Shippers and Dispatchers, creating delays, opacity, and frequent errors.


This project introduced Editable Cost Estimates, enabling controlled, transparent edits without breaking the underlying rate logic.

Designed an editable pricing system that cut revision time by ~70%, reduced pricing errors, and scaled across platforms without compromising rate integrity.

Role: Sole Product Designer
Platforms: Web (Dispatcher-focused)
Stakeholders: VP of Product, 2 PMs, Web + Mobile Engineers, Backend Engineer
Duration: Design sprint (2 days)

Problem

Problem

Problem

The existing flow was rigid and inefficient

Pseudo Lock

Shippers couldn’t correct mistakes in shipment details once an estimate was generated

One-Off Hacks

Dispatchers had to tweak rate charts for one-off changes, an anti-pattern.

Zero Transperacy

Discounts and surcharges were applied manually, with poor visibility into what changed

Error-Prone

Slab-based pricing made adjustments mathematically complex and error-prone

Operational Chaos

Every edit required tickets, messages, and recalculations

As operations scaled and competition increased, speed, accuracy, and ease of business became critical.

Design Goals

Design Goals

Design Goals

Enable safe, controlled edits without altering rate charts

Maintain 100% transparency in how costs are calculated

Reduce back-and-forth between Shippers and Dispatchers

Handle slab-based pricing without overwhelming the UI

Keep the experience fast, forgiving, and low-error

Key Constraints

Key Constraints

Key Constraints

1

Some fields originate from the order request form and cannot be changed

2

Slab-based charges needed to be editable, including adding new slabs

3

High information density had to fit into a single interaction surface

Process

Process

Process

What began as solution discussions quickly evolved into a design sprint.

Research & Inspiration

  • Benchmarked systems dealing with complex cost breakdowns and editable estimates

  • Mapped all estimate fields to understand editability and dependencies

  • Studied Excel as a mental model: complex logic, minimal interface

Rapid Exploration

  • Used Figma Make for fast wire-framing and iteration

  • Layered real data into early designs to expose edge cases early

  • Iterated closely with PMs and engineers to validate feasibility

Initial Information architecture in existing design style

Solution

Solution

Solution

A single, structured popup that balances power with clarity.

Core Capabilities

  • Inline editing of eligible fields

  • Editable slab-based pricing with add/remove slabs

  • Clear breakdown of every charge and calculation

  • Real-time cost summary

  • Save as Draft or Final before updating the Dispatcher

  • Dispatcher retains full edit control on their side

Interaction Design

  • Information is revealed progressively, not all at once

  • “Edit” CTA unlocks the cost table, keeping the default view clean

  • Contextual helper text reduces cognitive load and errors

  • Every number has a visible reason

This mental model helped structure grids, spacing, and hierarchy consistently and scaled cleanly across scenarios. It also made collaboration with engineers faster and clearer.

Design Framework

Design Framework

Design Framework

I treated information density like city planning.

Low-density rows for simple, single-value charges

Medium-density rows for grouped values

High-density, multi-story rows for slab-based pricing

Wireframing Outputs

I designed the prompt accordingly as started in Figma Make. The current iteration will undergoing some changes and improvements in the upcoming sprint while we are designing the high fidelity designs in Figma for all the products and personas.

Impact & Results

Impact & Results

Impact & Results

Metrics are directional and based on observed operational workflows.

Operational Efficiency

  • Reduced estimate edit cycles from multiple back-and-forth interactions to a single flow

  • Enabled Dispatchers to finalize revisions in minutes instead of hours or days

  • Eliminated the need to modify rate charts for one-off adjustments

Accuracy & Error Reduction

  • Removed manual math from discounting, surcharges, and slab edits

  • Prevented accidental pricing inconsistencies caused by rate chart tweaks

  • Introduced field-level visibility for every price and calculation

Support & Ops Load

  • Cut ticket volume related to estimate corrections by ~40–50%

  • Reduced dependency on ops teams for minor pricing changes

  • Lowered cognitive load for Dispatchers handling high quote volumes

Trust & Transparency

  • Made pricing changes explicit, traceable, and auditable

  • Improved shipper confidence by clearly showing what changed and what stayed fixed

  • Reduced pricing disputes and clarification requests

Platform Scalability

  • Designed a reusable interaction model now replicated across web and mobile

  • Supports new pricing rules and charge types without redesigning the system

  • Established a consistent mental model for cost editing across products
