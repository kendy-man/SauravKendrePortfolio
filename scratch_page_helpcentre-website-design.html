Help Centre

Help Centre

Overview

Overview

Overview

The Help Center is designed to be the single, comprehensive knowledge base for all features and process flows within our SaaS logistics platform.


Our primary goal is to provide an exceptional user experience by ensuring that users can find the information they need with minimum friction and fewer steps. This focus on efficiency is critical, as detailed, self-serve information helps our users, who spend significant hours using the system, to quickly resolve issues and optimize their work.

Approach

Approach

Approach

The redesign was specifically focused on restructuring the content presentation on the landing page to enhance discoverability and reduce friction, all while maintaining the integrity of the existing backend and article database architecture.


Design Process

Design Process

Design Process

The new design involved extensive iteration (approximately 10 design experiences) and rigorous testing to optimize the user flow. Initial AI driven wireframes to Figma high fidelity designs and dev hand over.

Goal

Goal

Goal

To deliver a refined and intuitive experience that ensures users quickly locate the necessary information. I used AI extensively. especially, Figma Make, Loveble.app

Issues with the Previous Design

The recent redesign was essential to overcome several significant drawbacks of the legacy documentation system, which hindered user efficiency and discoverability

Cluttered and Unstructured

The previous design lacked a clear content hierarchy, leading to a disorganized user experience.

Poor Discoverability

Users faced a complicated and unintuitive experience, making it difficult to find required information quickly.

Aesthetic and Usability

The overall design was not appealing, and article pages were described as "boring."

Aesthetic and Usability

Excessive use of digital real estate, particularly in headings, reduced space for content.

Inefficient Navigation

The number of steps required to navigate or jump between related articles was too high, slowing down the user.

Lack of Product Segmentation

Content was not categorized by specific product or feature area, making it hard for users to target information relevant to their immediate task (e.g., differentiating between Dispatching articles and Reporting articles).

Challenge

Traditional wireframing often leads to lengthy, inefficient feedback cycles and delayed stakeholder buy-in.

Solution

To mitigate this, I introduced an AI-powered, synchronous design session. Using lovable.app to generate the preliminary help center landing page wireframe, I then immediately facilitated a live review and refinement session with stakeholders. This direct, hands-on collaboration dramatically reduced project friction, condensing what is typically several days of back-and-forth communication into a highly productive 3-4 hour sprint. The result was instantaneous foundational approval and a validated starting point for the high-fidelity design phase.

Next Steps

Next Steps

Next Steps

Following the successful, synchronized wireframing phase, we transitioned to developing the high-fidelity designs in Figma. The entire process remained anchored in a user-centric design (UCD) philosophy, while strictly integrating the feedback and functional consensus achieved during the stakeholder workshop.


We embarked on an iterative visual exploration, creating various design treatments for the help center. This involved extensive visual experimentation with structural layouts and a deliberate process of color palette testing to optimize brand alignment, accessibility, and navigational clarity. This phase ensured that the final visual execution was not only aesthetically polished but also fully validated against both user needs and stakeholder objectives.

Finalizing the Visual System and Design Language

Finalizing the Visual System and Design Language

Finalizing the Visual System and Design Language

After a series of high-fidelity iterations, the core visual identity was finalized, establishing a comprehensive Design System foundation. We created the full design with support of responsiveness with providing designs for proper breakpoints.

Color Selection Rationale

The selection of Purple was deliberate, chosen for its strong brand alignment, as it successfully conveys attributes like youthfulness, vibrancy, technological advancement, and a premium quality. This primary color became the unifying visual element around which the entire product ecosystem was designed.

Typography Strategy

We selected Open Sans as the primary typeface. This decision was driven by its open-source licensing and robust support for multiple language sets, ensuring broad global compatibility and cost-effectiveness. Noto Sans was designated as the fall-back font to guarantee rendering consistency across diverse operating environments.

Developer Handoff

To ensure seamless implementation, a detailed Style Guide (or Design Token System) was created. This guide formalized all color codes, typographic scales, and component specifications, directly addressing developer needs for accuracy, scalability, and efficient implementation.

Quality Assurance and Successful Deployment

Quality Assurance and Successful Deployment

Quality Assurance and Successful Deployment

Following the completion of development, a comprehensive series of User Acceptance Tests (UATs) were conducted. The primary objective of the UAT phase was to rigorously validate that the functional implementation precisely matched the approved high-fidelity designs and met all critical functional requirements. We focused intensely on ensuring optimal usability while guaranteeing the delivery of top-notch quality and stability.


All testing protocols, bug tracking, and overall project management—including issue resolution and task assignment—were systematically tracked and managed using JIRA.


Project Launch: The completed help center was successfully launched and made live last week. This initial release delivered the core documentation and navigational structure.


Next Steps: Future enhancements are already in the roadmap, with plans to introduce the integrated AI chatbot functionality in the upcoming release cycle to further enhance self-service capabilities.
